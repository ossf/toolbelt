Welcome to the OpenSSF’s Securityology card game, a fast-paced game where you can learn about all the amazing work that happens within the Foundation.

The Game can be played several ways, with the goal being engaging with the players, informing them of the activities of the OpenSSF and how our groups and projects work together.

## The Basics



The game is composed of 5 (soon to be 7) unique decks of cards, each containing specific information about the TI, concept, or lifecycle phases detailed.  The decks are colour-coded to group the subjects.
Light Purple with Gold backs - This deck has all of the OpenSSF TI Logos.
Dark Purple with Gold backs - This deck has off the names and short descriptions of our TIs.
Light Blue with Dark Blue backs - The deck holds the stages of a Software Development Lifecycle (SDLC).
Gold with Purple backs - These cards detail each of the OpenSSF’s software and specification projects.
Dark Blue with Light Blue backs - These cards detail desired Security Capabilities that both maintainers and downstream consumers should seek out. 
Red and Gold backs - (forthcoming add-on) - These cards like common Threats that the assorted TIs help protect against.
Gold with Red backs - (forthcoming add-on) - These cards detail notorious cybersecurity Incidents or Vulnerabilities that can be matched up with the TIs and concepts to protect against.

There are very few “wrong” answers, the goal is engagement, not necessarily racking up points (although the game can be played that way as well if desired).
Straight-Forward Rules (the Matching Game)
Shuffle the cards and deal out the cards into their 5 coloured stacks.  
Pick a category to key off of, flip all of the cards of that type into a column.
Take turns flipping cards from the other categories and attempt to correctly match them with the original category.  Some cards will fit with several categories.
Illustrate Concepts
Separate and shuffle each stack, keeping the categories apart.
Pick SDLC or Security Concepts and deal out all cards of that category.
Take turns picking either TI Logos or Project cards and align them with the original chosen category.
Discuss why the cards are or are not good matches.

# Discovery Mode
## TI
Separate and shuffle each stack, keeping the categories apart.
Pick TI Logos and deal out all the cards in a column.
Deal out the TI Name + TL/DR cards.
Take turns matching the TI to the more detailed information.

## Project
Separate and shuffle each stack, keeping the categories apart.
Deal out all the Project cards into a column.
Deal out all the TI Logo cards into a column.
Take turns matching the TI Logos with the Projects they contain.

## Attack & Defend (forthcoming)
Separate and shuffle each stack, keeping the categories apart.
Choose a role: Blue Team Defender or Red Team Attacker.  Take the following decks:
Defender - TI Logos and Projects decks.
Attacker - Threats deck.
Deal out the Incidents and Vulnerabilities deck into a column between the players.  These will be bonus points that can be earned through the course of the playthrough.
Decide who will go first (e.g. the last person to have a PR merged) and alternate turns playing a card with the opposing party reacting to it.
The Incidents and Vulnerabilities are available for either party to claim after a card has been attacked/defended if they illustrate either the attack being carried out or if the technique/TI would help defend against it.
Claim any cards “won” into a pile.  At the end of the game, each card will equal one point.
Whomever has the most points claims victory and the accolades of all observers.
